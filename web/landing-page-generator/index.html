The cash-flow trap

The ad account says “scale.” The bank account says “absolutely not.”

This is the weird, expensive middle zone of ecommerce growth: sales are coming in, campaigns look promising, and the dashboard is flashing green — but cash is still not where you need it, when you need it.

Because revenue is not cash. And ROAS is not payroll, inventory, supplier deposits, refund reserves, card payments, shipping bills, or marketplace payout timing.

Illustrative example: Spend $2,000/day on ads for 7 days and that is $14,000 out before you fully understand how payouts, refunds, inventory, supplier timing, and fees hit your cash position.

Sound familiar?

  • You increase ad spend, but cash from sales lands days later.
  • Inventory needs to be reordered before the last batch fully pays you back.
  • Supplier invoices do not care that your ad dashboard looks pretty.
  • Refunds, chargebacks, and fees quietly shrink what you thought you had.
  • You can be “profitable” on paper and still short on cash this week.
  • The spreadsheet has become a crime scene.

FAQ

Questions from people who do not worship at the altar of ROAS.

Is this financial advice?

No. The calculator is for planning and analysis. It does not replace a CFO, CPA, accountant, tax professional, attorney, lender, or underwriter.

Will it tell me whether I should borrow money?

No. It can help you see whether cash timing may justify reviewing funding options, but it does not tell you to borrow or claim funding is the right move.

Does it guarantee I’ll qualify for funding?

No. Funding approval, rates, terms, and eligibility depend on the provider’s own review and requirements.

Does it guarantee my ads are profitable?

No. The calculator does not guarantee profitability, ad performance, revenue, or margin. It depends on your actual economics and inputs.

Can I use it without uploading files?

Yes. You can enter assumptions manually. Uploading spreadsheets, CSVs, payout reports, or ad exports may help provide better context.

Is this just another ROAS calculator?

No. ROAS is only one piece. This calculator focuses on cash timing: payouts, ad bills, inventory, supplier payments, refunds, fees, and working capital pressure.

What platforms is it for?

It is designed for ecommerce operators, including Shopify merchants, Amazon sellers, DTC brands, and paid-media teams. You should verify assumptions based on your actual platform, payment processor, and operational setup.
